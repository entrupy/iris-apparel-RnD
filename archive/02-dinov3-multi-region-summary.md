**Key insights:**

- **Majority voting is the best precision-recall tradeoff.** At the 2% FPR target,
it achieves 56.1% TPR using only 0.94% of the FPR budget -- nearly matching the
best single region while using less than half the allowed FPR.
- **All-must-agree maximizes recall** (78% TPR@2% target) but at the cost of FPR
rising to 5.4% -- unacceptable if the FPR budget is strict.
- **At the 5% FPR target, majority voting (65.9% TPR at 4.0% FPR) beats every
single region** while staying comfortably within the budget.
- **care_label alone remains the strongest single region** (60.6% TPR@2%), but
majority voting across all 4 regions is strictly better at every FPR budget.
- **care+logo 2-region voting** controls FPR better (2.31%) with 58.5% TPR, but
coverage is limited (only 61-68% of sessions have both regions).
